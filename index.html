What is Vedic Numerology (Ankaśāstra)?

Vedic numerology — Ankaśāstra — is the ancient Indian science of numbers rooted in Jyotish astrology. Unlike Western numerology, every digit corresponds to one of nine Jyotish planets and influences your personality, destiny, and life cycles.

Moolank Calculator — Your Birth Number

Your Moolank (birth number or root number) is derived by reducing your birth day to a single digit (1–9). Each planet governs a number: Sun (1), Moon (2), Jupiter (3), Rahu (4), Mercury (5), Venus (6), Ketu (7), Saturn (8), Mars (9). Use our free Moolank calculator above to find yours instantly.

Bhagyank Calculator — Your Destiny Number

The Bhagyank (destiny number or life path number) is found by summing all digits of your complete birth date and reducing to a single digit. It governs your career, destiny, and spiritual direction. Our free Bhagyank calculator shows this alongside detailed Jyotish interpretation.

Mahadasha — The 100-Year Vedic Planetary Cycle

The Mahadasha is a major planetary period in Vedic numerology. The cycle starts from your Moolank, moves through planets 1–9, with each planet ruling for years equal to its number (Sun = 1 year, Moon = 2 years … Mars = 9 years). Our free Mahadasha calculator shows your complete 100-year cycle.

Antardasha — Yearly Sub-Periods

Each calendar year carries an Antardasha sub-ruler computed from your birth year, Moolank, birth month, and the weekday of that year's birthday — all reduced to a single digit. This reveals the planetary influence governing each year of your life.

The Sacred Vedic Numerology Grid

The 3×3 Vedic grid uses fixed positions: 3-1-9 (divine/celestial row), 6-7-5 (material/earthly row), 2-8-4 (ancestral row). Digits from your birth date are placed in their respective positions. Repeated digits intensify that planet's vibration; absent numbers indicate areas requiring conscious development.
